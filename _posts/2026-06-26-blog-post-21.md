Preface
======
For the past several years, I've had countless conversations with aspiring and experienced Product Managers.

Some were trying to land their first PM role.

Some wanted to transition from engineering, design, or business into product management.

Others were already Product Managers but felt they had knowledge gaps or wanted to prepare for interviews.

Although their backgrounds were different, almost everyone struggled with the same challenge:

> **Where should I start, and how do I stay consistent?**

There are thousands of articles, books, YouTube videos, podcasts, online courses, newsletters, and frameworks available today. Ironically, having too many resources often makes learning harder instead of easier.

People spend hours searching for the "best" learning material instead of actually learning.

That observation inspired me to build something different.

## Introducing PM Learning

I created a learning platform that delivers **one structured lesson every day**, accompanied by practical exercises and progress tracking.

Instead of overwhelming learners with hundreds of topics at once, the platform encourages steady, consistent improvement through bite-sized daily learning.

The goal isn't to finish a course as quickly as possible.

The goal is to build knowledge that actually sticks.

## Why Daily Learning?

Most of us are busy.

Between work, meetings, family, side projects, and life in general, finding several uninterrupted hours to study is difficult.

However, finding 20–40 minutes each day is much more realistic.

Learning consistently has several advantages:

* It creates a habit.
* It reduces cognitive overload.
* It improves long-term retention.
* It makes progress visible.
* It feels achievable.

Small improvements every day compound into significant expertise over time.

## What You'll Find

Each day contains carefully selected learning material focused on a specific Product Management topic.

The lessons are designed to be practical rather than purely theoretical.

Alongside the learning content, you'll also find exercises that encourage you to think critically and apply what you've learned.

Reading is important.

Practicing is what turns knowledge into skill.

## Learning Progress

One feature I particularly wanted was progress tracking.

Many learning platforms simply provide content and leave motivation entirely up to the learner.

I wanted learners to clearly see how far they've come.

The platform keeps track of your learning journey so you can monitor your progress over time.

Seeing your completed lessons provides an extra source of motivation and makes it easier to stay consistent.

## Why I Built It

As someone who has worked in Product Management for years, I've learned from many different sources:

* books,
* mentors,
* online courses,
* real-world experience,
* product failures,
* successful launches,
* interviews,
* and conversations with other Product Managers.

One thing became clear:

Learning Product Management isn't about memorizing frameworks.

It's about developing a way of thinking.

Frameworks are helpful.

Templates are useful.

But the most valuable skill is learning how to identify problems, understand users, evaluate trade-offs, prioritize effectively, and make informed decisions.

I wanted to create a platform that helps develop that mindset.
